The Great Falls Race

Saturday, September 10, 2016

Race registration coming soon!

The festival's most dramatic and dazzling event is the Great Falls Race. This downriver race is a quick, adrenaline-packed descent of 60 vertical feet through the Potomac's spectacular class V+ Great Falls. Watch top kayakers from around the nation dash down the falls and compete for the Great Falls Race trophy. RACERS MUST REGISTER ONLINE PRIOR TO 5:00 PM ON JULY 10. THERE WILL BE NO RACE-DAY REGISTRATIONS. The exact course depends on the water level, but will take place on one of the following:

Maryland Lines

Course starts on river left shore just left of the large rock in the middle of the channel above Pummel. Racers will navigate Pummel and then choose to either go through Pencil Sharpener or Z-turn. Racers will then navigate Horseshoe and finish at a location explained at the competitors meeting.

Virginia Lines

Course starts on the river right (Virginia) shore just upstream of the large square slab rock. Racers then choose from the multiple lines on U-Hole and then navigate S-bend and The Spout. In the Qualifying round, the finish is a line from rock outcropping below the Spout to the the furthest downstream point of the Flake. In the Finals, the finish will be moved downstream into O-Deck rapid The locations will be explained at the competitors meeting.

Center Lines

The start is on the Virginia shore (river right) just upstream of the large square slab rock. Racers will ferry above the VA lines and navigate Grace Under Pressure. Racers then navigate the ledges rapid leading to The Fingers. The finish line will be a line from the Maryland shore to the Flake 50 yards downstream of the Fingers and Horseshoe, and the finish line may be moved downstream into O-Deck for the finals. The finish lines will be discussed at the competitors meeting.

Fishladder

The Fish Ladder race course starts in an eddy/cove upstream of the first slide on river left along the C&O canal towpath. Racers must navigate the first two slides or the sneak in tight quarters channel. Racers then will enter the crux of the run, where they will navigate a pre-determined line: either slides 3 and 4, the Back Canyon, or BiTc# Monkey, TBD at the race meeting. The finish will be below the fifth(last) slide at a location explained at the competitors meeting. The cut off for a fish ladder race is 4.8 feet.

Recommendations and Notes for Spectators

The final course will determine if it will be possible to view the race from both Great Falls, VA and the Great Falls Overlook, C&O National Historical Park, MD. Check back here the week of the race for a final recommendation. We encourage spectators to view the race from one of the 3 overlooks at Great Falls, VA. There is only one overlook at the C&O National Historical Park (Great Falls, MD)

Plan to arrive early—depending on the number of park visitors and spectators, there can be a line that forms at the entrance to the park. In addition, some of the prime viewing areas fill up quickly.

National Park Fee—There is a $10 fee to enter the park. On sunny days there can be a line to get into the park. Please plan accordingly. Note that, effective June 1st, 2015 the 3 day entrance fee is no longer valid for both the C&O Canal and Great Falls Park, VA so you will need to pay separate entry fees if you plan to visit both parks.
